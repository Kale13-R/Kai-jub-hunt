New-grad search tips

  • Search by license, not just title. Try “LMSW”, “LSW”, or “ASW” as keywords — postings that list the provisional license are, by definition, open to new grads.
  • “Case manager” is the biggest entry door. Hospitals, nonprofits, and county agencies hire far more case managers than “social workers,” and most only require the MSW.
  • County and state government hires constantly (child welfare, public mental health) and rarely requires experience — check the government boards below and the Employers tab.
  • Your field placements count as experience. List practicum hours and settings on your resume like jobs — agencies expect it from MSW grads.
  • Set alerts. Once a search looks right on Indeed/LinkedIn, save it as a daily email alert so postings come to you.

Licensing, state by state

Louisiana is done ✅ — you hold an active LMSW. This tab now tracks keeping it current, the path up to LCSW, and how to add Illinois or California if a role in those regions actually comes through.

🎷 Louisiana — LMSW in hand ✅ (next: LCSW)
  • You have this: Licensed Master Social Worker (LMSW), Louisiana State Board of Social Work Examiners — License #19787, expires 08/2026.
  • Renewal: LMSW renews annually — calendar a reminder well before 08/2026 to renew and confirm you’ve completed the current continuing education (CE) requirements. Check labswe.org for the exact CE hours and renewal steps — don’t rely on last year’s numbers.
  • Next step, LCSW: accrue supervised clinical practice hours under a Board-Approved Clinical Supervisor (BACS), then sit for the ASWB Clinical exam. Verify the current required hour count and supervision structure on labswe.org before committing to a supervisor.
  • Practical win: with the LMSW active right now, you already qualify for any posting that says “LMSW required” — that’s a large slice of hospital and agency case-management roles. Apply to those immediately, no waiting.
  • Board: labswe.org
🌆 Illinois — LSW by endorsement (then LCSW)
  • You’re not starting from scratch: you already hold an active out-of-state LMSW and have passed the ASWB Master’s exam. Apply to IDFPR for LSW licensure by endorsement using your Louisiana license, rather than reapplying as a new grad.
  • Your ASWB Master’s exam score can typically be transferred to Illinois through aswb.org instead of retaking the exam — confirm the transfer process and any endorsement paperwork directly with IDFPR.
  • Chicago Public Schools still requires a separate PEL with a school social worker endorsement (ISBE) for school SW positions — nonprofits, hospitals, and DCFS do not.
  • LCSW in Illinois has its own supervised clinical hour requirement plus the ASWB Clinical exam — check current numbers with IDFPR rather than assuming they match Louisiana’s.
  • Board: idfpr.illinois.gov
🌴 California — ASW registration (then LCSW)
  • This path is unchanged by holding the LMSW — CA’s ASW registration is degree-based, not license-based. Register with the CA Board of Behavioral Sciences as an ASW (Associate Clinical Social Worker) using your Tulane MSW. No exam needed to register — just the degree and application.
  • Your ASWB Master’s exam pass does not shortcut California’s clinical track — CA requires its own supervised-hours path to LCSW plus the CA Law & Ethics exam and the ASWB Clinical exam at the end.
  • Since ASW registration only needs the degree, this is still a fast, straightforward step to add whenever a CA opportunity looks real.
  • Tons of LA/OC/SD county and nonprofit postings say “ASW required or eligible” — those are open to you as soon as you register.
  • Board: bbs.ca.gov
⚠️ Requirements change — always verify current rules, fees, CE hours, and supervised-hour counts on the board sites before applying or renewing. If you might move between these states, lean on endorsement/reciprocity from your active Louisiana license rather than starting over.
