New-grad search tips

  • Search by license, not just title. Try “LMSW”, “LSW”, or “ASW” as keywords — postings that list the provisional license are, by definition, open to new grads.
  • “Case manager” is the biggest entry door. Hospitals, nonprofits, and county agencies hire far more case managers than “social workers,” and most only require the MSW.
  • County and state government hires constantly (child welfare, public mental health) and rarely requires experience — check the government boards below and the Employers tab.
  • Your field placements count as experience. List practicum hours and settings on your resume like jobs — agencies expect it from MSW grads.
  • Set alerts. Once a search looks right on Indeed/LinkedIn, save it as a daily email alert so postings come to you.

Licensing, state by state

The credential you can get right after graduating is what unlocks most postings. Quick map for your three target regions:

🎷 Louisiana — LMSW (then LCSW)
  • Right after your MSW: apply to the LA State Board of Social Work Examiners for LMSW (Licensed Master’s Social Worker) — requires the MSW and passing the ASWB Master’s exam. No work experience needed.
  • With the LMSW you can practice under supervision, which covers most agency, hospital, and case-management jobs.
  • LCSW comes later, after several years of board-approved supervised clinical hours and the ASWB Clinical exam.
  • Board: labswe.org
🌆 Illinois — LSW (then LCSW)
  • Right after your MSW: apply to IDFPR for the LSW (Licensed Social Worker) — with an MSW, no supervised experience is required; you pass the ASWB Master’s exam.
  • Chicago Public Schools requires a separate PEL with a school social worker endorsement (ISBE) for school SW positions — but nonprofits, hospitals, and DCFS do not.
  • LCSW requires ~3,000 supervised clinical hours post-MSW plus the ASWB Clinical exam.
  • Board: idfpr.illinois.gov
🌴 California — ASW registration (then LCSW)
  • Right after your MSW: register with the CA Board of Behavioral Sciences as an ASW (Associate Clinical Social Worker). No exam needed to register — just the degree and application.
  • ASW registration lets you accrue the ~3,000 supervised hours (over at least 104 weeks) needed for LCSW; you’ll also take the CA Law & Ethics exam during your first renewal cycle and the ASWB Clinical exam at the end.
  • Tons of LA/OC/SD county and nonprofit postings say “ASW required or eligible” — those are new-grad jobs.
  • Board: bbs.ca.gov
⚠️ Requirements change — always verify current rules, fees, and hour counts on the board sites before applying. If you might move between these states, apply for the home-state license first; transferring an active license is easier than starting over.
💡 Put “LMSW candidate” / “ASW-eligible” on your resume the moment you apply for the license. Recruiters filter for those exact strings.
